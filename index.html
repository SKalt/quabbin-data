Water levels in the Quabbin reservoir

As the largest inland body of water in Massachusetts, the Quabbin reservoir acts as a barometer for the rainfall in the state. Given the recent drought, the downward trend in water levels from 2007/8 through 2017 seems worrying. It will likely take years to rebuild water levels to full capacity, but the possibility of a persistent decrease in rainfalll is more worrying.

Fortunately, comparing water levels across years does not reveal an obvious trend. Due to the chaotic nature of weather, it is reasonable to attribute the 2016-7 drought to chance. More water level data might reveal a change in the Quabbin's luck.

The Massachusetts Water Resource Authority is tasked with monitoring water levels in the Quabbin and across the state, and has generously allowed me to publish this data. If you would like to use the data, you can download it here; the citation is in the README.
